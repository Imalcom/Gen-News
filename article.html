Comparing Johari’s Window and Small Group Dynamics

The Johari Window, created by Luft and Ingham (1955), focuses on self-awareness and interpersonal communication through four quadrants: Open, Blind, Hidden, and Unknown. It fosters trust and collaboration.

In contrast, Small Group Dynamics centers on interpersonal relationships, cohesion, and conflict resolution within teams. It emphasizes flexible and situational interactions to achieve goals.

Real-Life Applications of Johari’s Window

  • Therapy: Couples enhance communication by reducing hidden/blind areas.
  • Peer Support: Alcoholic recovery groups build trust through openness.
  • Education: Teacher-student relationships improve via transparency.
  • Corporate Use: Companies like Microsoft and Google use it to foster feedback-driven cultures and improve teamwork.

This model proves valuable in personal growth, conflict resolution, and team development, supporting a more cohesive civilization.
